Healthcare Vs. Poverty by State

Correlations Discovered Between Income and Access to Healthcare

In this activity I created an interactive graph using D3 to plot the correlations between poverty level and access to healthcare by state. Upon hovering over the markers, we can see the percentage of that states population that is in poverty and lacks healthcare.

This graph gives us a good visualization of the states that struggle most to meet the healthcare needs of the most vulnerable people in our society. This visualization could be used by states to compare their policies regarding healthcare provision and support for those in poverty to others who may have more robust social support systems in place.

At a glance, we can see that the states with higher levels of poverty and higher levels of people without access to healthcare tend to be the states with lower average income levels. Most of these states are located in the Southeast. We can also see that most of the states with lower levels of poverty and lower levels of people without access to healthcare tend to be the states with higher average income levels. Most of these states are located in the Northeast. States with moderate levels of poverty and lack of access to healthcare tend to be in the Midwest.
